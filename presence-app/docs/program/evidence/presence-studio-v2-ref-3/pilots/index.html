How a pilot is built

Each room is its own world — own type, own palette, own motion vocabulary, own page architecture. No shared CSS, no template chrome. The brand of the person is the brand of the room.

Pages can extend beyond two: a gallery could grow a studio archive; a DJ booth could grow a tour calendar; a workshop could grow a process journal. The threshold and the first chamber are the floor.

The studio cockpit (editor) lives at a separate layer — preview the editor view — and never expresses personality. It frames the room's personality.

Quality checks before we ship

  • Image-first · type-loud · the person inhabits this place
  • At least one canvas effect — grain, noise, waveform, fibres, particles
  • At least one piece of named motion — spotlight, marquee, vinyl, dust, glitch
  • Scrolling beyond the fold is the same person, not a generic footer
  • The room is alive before anyone enters it — atmospheric, breathing, worth entering
